Top FAQs

What is the EEO-1 Report?
The EEO-1 Component 1 Report is a compliance data collection mandated by Federal Statute and Regulations (Civil Rights Act of 1964, as amended). The data collection requires employers to report the number of employees categorized by race/ethnicity, gender and job category.
What is the EEO-1 filing deadline?
The filing deadline is typically three months after data collection launches. The 2019 and 2020 EEO-1 Online Filing System is expected to launch in April 2021 and close in July 2021. The exact dates will be posted after data collection officially launches, and this FAQ will be updated.
What companies are required to file the EEO-1 report?
The following companies are required to file the EEO-1 report annually:
  1. Private employers with 100 or more employees.
  2. Federal government prime contractors or first-tier subcontractors subject to Executive Order 11246, as amended with both 50 or more employees and a prime contractor first-tier subcontract amounting to $50,000 or more.
  3. Companies that serve as a depository of Government funds in any amount or is a financial institution which is an issuing and paying agent for U.S. Savings Bonds and Savings Notes and have 50 or more employees.
Will companies need to file data for both 2019 and 2020 in April 2021?
Companies must file the EEO-1 report for each year they are in business and meet the filing requirements. In April 2021, eligible companies will be required to first file for 2019, then file for 2020 after the 2019 report is submitted.
Can filers use login information from EEO-1 Component 2?
No. The Component 2 account information cannot be used to access the EEO-1 Component Online Filing System. These are two different data collections, using distinct systems.

How do employers determine an employee’s race and/or ethnicity?
Employers are required to offer employees an opportunity to self-identify for the purposes of the EEO-1 report. This information can be collected on applications or a voluntary survey. The employer would collect the data voluntarily and aggregate this information into the EEO-1 report. When collecting information, employers should notify employees that providing this information is voluntary.
How do employers classify employees into the job categories listed in the EEO-1 report?

The job categories are based on employee skill level, knowledge and responsibilities and are designed to reflect common company hierarchies. Please select the category that best reflects employees in the company.

To help, there is a job classification guide that maps Standard Occupational Classifications (SOC) and Census Job Codes to the job classifications, which can be accessed here.

Detailed descriptions of the job classifications can also be found in the instructional booklet, which can be accessed here.

For a summary guide of the job classifications, please visit the fact sheet on job categories here.

What are the options for submitting the EEO-1 Report?
The preferred and most efficient method of submitting Component 1 EEO-1 reports is through the EEO-1 Online Filing System or as an electronically transmitted datafile (ASCII/TEXT or CSV/XML file) via a data file upload. The filer support page on the website will include data file upload instructions in April 2021, when data collection launches. Specifications will be similar to previous years. For a summary of the data employers will need to collect, please see the factsheets on Preparing Data – Single-establishment here, or Preparing Data – Multi-establishment here.
If a company has never filed an EEO-1 Report before, how can the filer get started?

Once the registration system is open, navigate to the registration page, and follow the instructions. After submitting the registration form, file the company number and password that is provided.

Registration is currently not open. Please submit contact information on the contact page here to receive updates when the registration system is open.
