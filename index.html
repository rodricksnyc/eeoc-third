2019 & 2020 EE0-1 Data Collection

The Employer Information Report EEO-1 Component 1, also known as the EEO-1 Component 1 Report, will begin data collection in April and May 2021. Organizations can file through the online filing system.

Have a Question?

Top FAQs

What is the EEO-1 Report?
The EEO-1 Component 1 Report is a compliance data collection mandated by Federal Statute and Regulations (Civil Rights Act of 1964, as amended). The data collection requires employers to report the number of employees categorized by race/ethnicity, gender and job category.
What is the EEO-1 filing deadline?
The filing deadline is typically three months after data collection launches. The 2019 and 2020 EEO-1 Data Collection is expected to launch in April 2021 and close in July 2021. The exact dates will be posted after data collection officially launches, and this FAQ will be updated.
What companies are required to file the EEO-1 report?
The following companies are required to file the EEO-1 report annually:
  1. Private employers with 100 or more employees.
  2. Federal government prime contractors or first-tier subcontractors subject to Executive Order 11246, as amended with both 50 or more employees and a prime contractor first-tier subcontract amounting to $50,000 or more.
  3. Companies that serve as a depository of Government funds in any amount or is a financial institution which is an issuing and paying agent for U.S. Savings Bonds and Savings Notes and have 50 or more employees.
Will companies need to file data for both 2019 and 2020 in April 2021?
Companies must file the EEO-1 report for each year they are in business and meet the filing requirements. In April 2021, eligible companies will be required to first file for 2019, then file for 2020 after the 2019 report is submitted.
Can filers use login information from EEO-1 Component 2?
No. The Component 2 account information cannot be used to access the EEO-1 Component Online Filing System. These are two different data collections, using distinct systems.

Who Must File

Find out if you need to file!

Answer the questions below to determine if your Company is required to file the EEO-1 report:

Does the company employ 100 or more employees?
Does the company have over 50 employees AND has a federal contract or subcontract amounting to $50,000 or more?
Does the company have over 50 employees AND serves as an issuing and paying agent for U.S. Savings Bonds?

Yes, you are required to report

Close

A. All private employers who are:

  1. subject to Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 (as amended by the Equal Employment Opportunity Act of 1972) with 100 or more employees EXCLUDING state and local governments, public primary and secondary school systems, institutions of higher education, American Indian or Alaska Native tribes and tax-exempt private membership clubs other than labor organizations;

    2. OR

  2. subject to Title VII who have fewer than 100 employees if the company is owned or affiliated with another company, or there is centralized ownership, control or management (such as central control of personnel policies and labor relations) so that the group legally constitutes a single enterprise, and the entire enterprise employs a total of 100 or more employees.

B. All federal contractors (private employers), who:

  1. are not exempt as provided for by 41 CFR 60-1.5,
  2. have 50 or more employees, and
    1. are prime contractors or first-tier subcontractors, and have a contract, subcontract, or purchase order amounting to $50,000 or more; or
    2. serve as a depository of Government funds in any amount, or
    3. a financial institution which is an issuing and paying agent for U.S. Savings Bonds and Savings Notes.

Only those establishments located in the District of Columbia and the 50 states are required to submit Standard Form 100. No reports should be filed for establishments in Puerto Rico, the Virgin Islands, or other American Protectorates.

What will I need?

  • FEIN
  • Contractor ASA
  • Employee counts by race, origin, and ex
  • Address and FEINs of all locations

Previous Filers

Filers should receive their company' annual notification letter by mail prior to the data collection opening.

Requirements

  • The requirement for providing the Employer Identification Number (FEIN) for each establishment location will be more carefully monitored.
  • Company establishments with the same address, NAICS Code and FEIN must be combined into one establishment record.
